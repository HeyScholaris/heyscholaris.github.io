Section 1

Sector Overview

The Far Sector is stable only in the way pressure can look like order.

The Far Sector is a contested region held together by freight lanes, surveillance grids, orbital industry, extractive law, and fear. The Galactic Union claims it brought stability after the old wars, and to many in the upper tiers that feels true. To workers, colonists, drifters, and lower-district communities, it often feels like a machine of control.

Order vs Survival

The Union values compliance, productivity, and control. Most ordinary people value food, protection, work, and not being crushed by the system.

Memory vs Propaganda

The truth about the old Psionic houses and sects survives in fragments, family stories, ruins, off-book archives, and forbidden records.

Cybernetics vs Spirit

Technology is everywhere, but not neutral. It empowers, tracks, standardizes, and wounds. Psionics remains one of the few forces the Union cannot fully industrialize.

Section 2

The Truth About Psionics

The public version is incomplete by design.

The hidden history

Long before the modern Union, Psionic users formed bloodlines, schools, dueling houses, pilgrimage sects, advisory circles, and mystic courts. Some were guardians and scholars. Some were self-serving elites. The Union did not merely regulate them. It systematically broke them.

How the Union destroyed them

  • undermined old institutions politically
  • isolated their schools and lines of succession
  • criminalized independent training
  • absorbed useful survivors into controlled programs
  • spread fear until public memory rewrote itself

What came after

  • captured or inducted Psionics were fitted with inhibitors and control systems
  • some became state assets, interrogators, black operatives, and shock troops
  • others were broken, hidden, or erased from public record
  • the official story reframed all of this as safety and stabilization
GM truth: older people may remember Psionics as noble, sacred, feared, or politically dangerous. Younger people usually know only the Union version. Black-market, rebel, and archive circles know enough to doubt it.
Section 3

Running the Union

The Union works best as believable power, not cartoon villainy.

How the privileged see it

  • the Union ended old wars
  • trade flows because someone keeps order
  • piracy, famine, and chaos are kept in check
  • Psionic oversight is unfortunate but necessary

How the lower sector sees it

  • the Union taxes and strips worlds bare
  • security means raids, disappearances, and suppression
  • law protects contracts more reliably than people
  • protest becomes sedition very quickly
Best practice: not every Union official should be cruel. Many are sincere, compromised, frightened, ambitious, or convinced harsh measures are the cost of stability. That makes them more useful than flat villains.
Section 4

Major Systems & Locations

The sector’s major arenas of power, industry, mystery, and collapse.

Cortega System

Cortega Prime: layered city-world of embassies, towers, barracks, lower-district survival, and buried infrastructure.

Halcyon Spire: pageantry, tribunals, parades, and public displays of Union unity.

Blackline Undercity: tunnels, scrap markets, shrines, gangs, and hidden records.

Station Crown: fleet docks, customs, checkpoints, diplomatic access, prisoner transfer lanes.

Lilith System

Greenreach: jungle frontier, colonial pressure, violent weather, and dangerous fauna.

Cinder Wake: mining world of fissures, quarry shelves, camps, and labor unrest.

Harrow Station: officially a survey site, unofficially a black-site rumor magnet.

Widow’s Belt: broken rigs, pirate caches, rebel docks, and ambush ground.

Indego System

Nacre: floating cities, thaw politics, memory traditions, and seasonal movement.

Duskmere: trench settlements, ice wrecks, glacial survival, and occult ruins.

Lantern Path: a seasonal route that drives ritual, logistics, and urgency.

Pilgrim Arrays: orbital structures tied to old mysteries and Psionic rumor.

Embargo System

Brass Meridian: industrial city-station of foundries, cyberware, labor barracks, and extreme class division.

The Cincture: linked factory stations building fleet hardware and weapons.

Ashfall: poisoned reclamation world where workers feel discarded by design.

Morrow’s Drop: a semi-secret freeport used by everyone and endorsed by no one.

Riftessar System

Redwake: mercenary port, exile market, and false-name haven.

Vanta Grave: salvage world of war debris and buried atrocities.

Hush Colony Nine: sanctuary, cover story, or structured lie, depending on who holds it.

The Far Choir: dead stations, echoing signals, and late-campaign weirdness.

Section 5

Core Factions & Organizations

The big actors players are likely to brush against or serve.

Galactic Union

The ruling military-bureaucratic power. Useful internal branches include Central Fleet, Domestic Order Bureau, Colonial Office, Industrial Provision Ministry, Tribunal Service, and the rumored Psionic Induction apparatus.

Rebel Forces

Not one army but a patchwork: Free Sector cells, worker fronts, pilgrim networks, defectors, smugglers, and local militias.

Bounty Hunters Guildship

Neutral contract institution on paper. In practice, it is a machine that makes freelance violence lawful and profitable.

Iron Choir

Union-aligned cybernetic veteran corps, enhancement program, or both. A symbol of heroic sacrifice to some and state terror to others.

Veil Market

A distributed underworld network trading in identities, cyberware, contraband, restricted relics, data, and routes.

Last Sects

The rumor-name for surviving Psionic circles, bloodlines, shrine schools, hidden teachers, and broken lineages.

Meridian Dynamics

A major Embargo industrial combine tied to cyberware, ship components, and military contracts. Legal, powerful, and deeply complicit.

Pilgrim Archive

Historians, oral keepers, relic guardians, and recorders of the truths official memory tried to erase.

Section 6

Character Origin Groups

Broad roots that tie PCs into the structure of the sector.

Union Born

Raised under formal schooling, bureaucracy, military culture, or official service.

Undercity Native

Shaped by lower-tier districts, gang lines, utility tunnels, clinics, and informal debt networks.

Frontier Colonist

Comes from outposts, mining settlements, thaw-cities, survey camps, or homesteads under pressure.

Guild Licensed

Tied to bounty work, transports, claims verification, restraint handling, or retrieval contracts.

Rebel Raised

Grew up around safehouses, organizers, fugitives, cell logistics, or anti-Union communities.

Corporate / Contract Class

Worked in industrial management, cyberware systems, registries, compliance, or logistics.

Shrine-Born / Archive-Taught

Raised near old teachings, relic vaults, sect remnants, or memory houses.

Driftborn Spacer

Home is shipboard, dockside, or route-based rather than world-based.

Section 7

Campaign Lenses

Strong default structures for campaigns in the Far Sector.

Small-Crew Rebellion

The crew are couriers, saboteurs, liberators, and deniable assets moving between cells that do not always trust one another.

Bounty & Contract Play

The crew hunt fugitives, recover targets, enforce warrants, and slowly discover that every contract is political.

Smuggler Survival

The crew moves medicine, fugitives, relics, forged papers, or contraband across blockades, checkpoints, and hunted routes.

Psionic Fugitive Story

One or more characters are tied to shattered sects, induction programs, old bloodlines, or forbidden teachings.

Industrial Noir

Focus on Embargo, Cortega, surveillance, cyberware, labor violence, corruption, and the cost of “order.”

Late-Campaign Mythic Escalation

Start grounded, then move toward lost records, forbidden arrays, black sites, ancient sect truths, and sector-scale revelation.

GM principle: the Far Sector works best when public truth, private truth, and lived truth are all slightly different. Let players discover the seams.
