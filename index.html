Section 1

What This Game Is

A familiar d20 chassis built for fast, cinematic sci-fantasy play.

This game is about

  • dangerous jobs in a fractured galaxy
  • small crews making a big difference
  • blasters, starships, and relic blades
  • rebellion cells and bounty contracts
  • cyberware, syndicates, and black markets
  • rare, dangerous users of Psionics

This ruleset uses

  • d20 + modifiers for almost everything
  • Advantage / Disadvantage for simple bonuses and penalties
  • Edge and Threat to make rolls cinematic
  • Callings and Paths for character identity
  • Talents for customization
  • Heat and Reputation to drive campaigns

Section 2

Core Rules

The basic roll, advantage, cinematic momentum, and contested actions.

The Basic Roll

When an action matters, roll 1d20 + Attribute + Skill. If the total meets or beats the target number, you succeed. You roll against either a Difficulty or an enemy's Defense.

DifficultyBenchmark
10Easy
12Standard
15Hard
18Extreme
22Legendary

Advantage and Disadvantage

  • Advantage: roll 2d20 and keep the higher.
  • Disadvantage: roll 2d20 and keep the lower.
  • If both apply, they cancel each other out.

Edge

Gain Edge when you succeed by 5 or more, or when you roll a natural 20.

Spend 1 Edge to deal +1d6 damage, move an extra short distance, disarm/shove/trip/pin a target, gain or improve cover, help an ally gain Advantage, reduce task time, avoid collateral damage, spot a useful detail, or keep a weapon from overheating or running dry.

Threat

The GM gains Threat when you fail by 5 or more, or when you roll a natural 1.

Use 1 Threat to introduce reinforcements, alarms, target repositioning, weapon failure, lockdowns, social suspicion, ship strain, or civilian danger. Threat should intensify scenes, not shut them down.

Contested Rolls

If two characters directly oppose each other, both roll 1d20 + relevant Attribute + relevant Skill. The higher result wins.

Hiding vs Notice Deceive vs Discipline Command vs Discipline Psionic vs Discipline Pilot vs Pilot

Character Creation

Create a character in six steps.

Step 1 — Assign Attributes

Assign these modifiers across the six Attributes:

+2+2+1+10−1
Step 2 — Choose a Background

A Background tells the group who you were before the campaign. It grants 2 trained skills, 1 contact, and 1 useful item or perk.

Step 3 — Choose a Calling

Your Calling is your main role. At level 1 it gives trained skills, starting features, and starting gear suggestions.

Step 4 — Choose Trained Skills

You begin with 2 trained skills from Background and 4 trained skills from Calling. If a skill would be gained twice, choose another from the same list.

Step 5 — Choose Starting Equipment

Take one primary weapon, one backup weapon or tool, one armor set, a field kit tied to your role, and one signature item from your background.

Section 5

Combat Rules

Fast rounds, simple movement, and cinematic tactical choices.

Each Round1 Action
Each Round1 Move
Each Round1 Reaction

Movement

  • across a small room
  • between pieces of cover
  • up a corridor segment
  • into melee with a nearby target
  • If using a grid, Move is 6 squares.

Equipping and Using Weapons

  • Light weapons: You may equip or draw the weapon and use it as part of the same Action.
  • Non-Light weapons: Equipping or readying the weapon takes your Action. Attacking or firing with it takes a separate Action.
  • Heavy weapons: Heavy weapons follow the non-light rule unless a feature says otherwise.
  • If a weapon is already in hand and ready, using it only takes the normal attack Action.

Common Actions

Attack Dash Aim Hide Assist Use Item Hack Stabilize Use Psionics Ready
Attack Roll: roll 1d20 + relevant Attribute + combat Skill and compare the result to the target's Defense. Melee uses Body or Agility + Melee. Ranged uses Agility + Ranged.

Damage and Critical Hits

  • On a hit, roll the weapon's damage.
  • On a natural 20, deal maximum normal damage, then roll the weapon damage dice again and add the result.
  • Optional critical effects: disarm target, knock target prone, shove target into cover, destroy carried gear, or inflict 1 extra Wound on a Downed target.

Cover

  • Half cover: +2 Defense
  • Full cover: +5 Defense
  • Targets behind full cover usually cannot be hit unless the attack can arc, explode, pierce, or ignore cover.
Range BandMeaning
Engagedclose enough for melee - (1-2 Grid)
Shortsame room or close lane - (6-12 Grid)
Mediumacross a street, hangar, or long hall - (12-24 Grid)
Longrooftop to rooftop, sniper line, open field - (>30 Grid)
Extremespecialized weapons or ship scale - (>100 Grid)

Aiming, Hiding, and Helping

  • Aim: If you spend your Action aiming, your next ranged attack before the end of your next turn has Advantage.
  • Hide: Roll Stealth against enemy Notice. If you stay hidden, enemies may lose track of you or suffer Disadvantage to attack you.
  • Assist: As an Action, make a clear effort to help an ally. If the GM agrees the help matters, that ally gains Advantage on one related roll.

Grappling

To grapple, roll Athletics vs the target's Athletics or Agility. On success, the target is Restrained until they break free, you move away, you are forced off, or you release them.

  • Restrained targets cannot move freely.
  • They have Disadvantage on Agility-based actions.
  • They are easier to capture.

Downed, Wounds, and Death

  • At 0 Health, you are Downed.
  • At the start of each of your turns, roll 1d20.
  • 10+: you hold on.
  • 9 or less: mark 1 Wound.
  • At 3 Wounds, you die.

Healing

  • An ally can stabilize you with Medicine 12, a medkit and a successful Medicine check, or a feature or Psionic power that restores Health.
  • Once stabilized, you remain unconscious until healed.
  • Medkits restore Health outside combat and may stabilize during combat.
  • Psionic healing restores Health directly.
  • A Full Rest restores all Health unless the GM rules a severe injury needs care.

Section 6

Psionic Rules

Resolve, Psionic powers, strain, and optional moral pressure.

Who Can Use Psionics

Only characters with Psionic training may take Psionics skill and use Psionic powers. In the core rules, that is normally the Adept Calling, though the GM may allow rare training or special talents for others.

Resolve

Resolve measures focus, spiritual control, and inner reserves. You spend Resolve to use Psionic powers and some special Calling features.

Starting Resolve3 + Will
Using a Psionic Power

Choose the power, spend the listed Resolve cost (usually 1), then roll 1d20 + Will + Psionic and compare to a target's Defense or a set Difficulty.

Psionic Strain

If you use a Psionic power while at 0 Resolve, you may still use it, but you take 1 Strain.

  • Each point of Strain gives −1 to Psionic rolls.
  • Strain remains until a Full Rest.
  • At 3 Strain, the GM may trigger a backlash.

Possible Backlash

  • a visible aura
  • emotional overflow
  • objects shaking or breaking
  • attracting hostile attention
  • local electronics glitching
  • revealing your location through Psionics
Psionic Powers
Push / Pull — Cost 1 Resolve: Move a nearby creature or object a short distance. Attack use: roll vs Defense. On a hit, deal 1d6 Psionic damage and push the target.
Heighten — Cost 1 Resolve: Gain Advantage on one Initiative, Notice, Agility, or Athletics roll this scene.
Sense — Cost 1 Resolve: Ask one question Psionics might answer, such as: Is danger near? Is there life nearby? Is someone hiding hostile intent? Has Psionics been used here recently? The GM should answer truthfully, though not always completely.
Leap — Cost 1 Resolve: Make an impossible jump or fall safely this turn.
Bind — Cost 1 Resolve: Roll Psionic vs target Defense or Discipline. On a success, the target is restrained until the end of your next turn.
Deflect — Cost 1 Resolve, Reaction: Reduce incoming ranged damage by 1d8 + Will. If damage is reduced to 0, you may redirect the shot at a valid target within Short range.
Mind Trick — Cost 1 Resolve: Roll Psionic vs target Discipline. On success, plant a short, plausible suggestion. The request cannot obviously compel self-destruction unless the GM allows a powerful narrative moment.
Surge — Cost 1 Resolve: Deal 1d8 energy damage to a nearby target, or add +1d8 to a melee attack this turn.
Heal — Cost 1 Resolve: Restore 1d8 + Will Health to a touched target. Each target can benefit from Heal once per Short Rest unless a feature says otherwise.
Cloak — Cost 1 Resolve: Gain Advantage on Stealth and become difficult to track until you attack, shout, or take obvious hostile action.
Psionic and Morality: This game does not require a fixed morality chart, but cruel, reckless, or fear-fueled Psionic use should feel dangerous. Optional rule: when a Psionic user acts through cruelty, domination, or rage, they gain 1 extra Strain after the power resolves.

Section 7

Cyberware Rules

Powerful implants with social and spiritual tradeoffs.

Cyber Load Limit2 + Tech
Over Limit Penalty−1 Presence
Over Limit Penalty−1 Discipline

Cyber Load

Each implant has a Load value. If your total Load exceeds your safe Cyber Load, for every point over the limit you suffer:

  • −1 Presence
  • −1 Discipline

The GM may also rule that some NPCs distrust heavily altered characters on sight.

Installing Cyberware

Installing cyberware requires access to the implant, proper tools, a qualified installer or facility, and time and money or barter. The GM may turn installation into downtime, a mission reward, or a black-market scene.

Cyberware and Psionics: Psionics still works through cybernetic bodies, but severe overload can disrupt emotional control. If a Psionic user is over their Cyber Load limit, they also take −1 Psionic for every 2 points over the limit, rounded down.

Hunter
Bounty hunters Trackers Mercenaries Pursuit specialists

Trained Skills: Ranged, Notice, Athletics, and one of Melee, Pilot, or Stealth.

  • Level 1 — Marked Target: As a bonus action, mark one visible target. Once per turn, deal +2 damage to that target.
  • Level 1 — Track Quarry: Gain Advantage on checks to locate, identify, follow, or profile a chosen target.
  • Level 5 — Relentless Pursuit: Your mark cannot gain the benefit of Half Cover against your attacks, and you ignore normal penalties for tracking through crowds, bad weather, or clutter.
  • Level 9 — No Escape: Once per scene, when your marked target moves away, you may immediately move after them or make a ranged attack as a Reaction.

Hunter Paths

Gunslinger — Level 3 Fast Hands: Draw, reload, or switch weapons for free once each turn.
Gunslinger — Level 7 Twin Shot: When using a Light pistol, you may make one off-hand shot at -2 as part of the same Action.
Gunslinger — Level 10 Dead Run Duelist: Once per scene, move, attack with Advantage, and then move again without provoking reactions.
Tracker — Level 3 Snare Expert: You carry or improvise traps. Once per scene, set a trap as an Action. A creature entering it must pass Athletics or Agility 14 or become slowed, restrained, or revealed.
Tracker — Level 7 Ghost on the Trail: Gain Advantage on Stealth and Notice while pursuing a quarry. Hidden enemies do not gain Advantage against you unless they beat your Notice by 5 or more.
Tracker — Level 10 Unerring Hunt: Once you have studied a quarry for at least one scene, the GM must tell you one of the following truthfully: where they would flee, what they fear, what protects them, or who they trust.
Bruiser — Level 3 Shock Entry: When you move into melee and hit a target, you may push or stagger them without spending Edge.
Bruiser — Level 7 Iron Frame: Gain +1 Armor or reduce one incoming physical hit by 2 each round.
Bruiser — Level 10 Bring Them In: When you reduce a target to 0 Health in melee, you may choose to automatically incapacitate instead of kill, even if the weapon would normally be lethal.
Operative
Spies Infiltrators Slicer-thieves Saboteurs Assassins

Trained Skills: Stealth, Deceive, Hacking, Notice.

  • Level 1 — Sneak Attack: Once per turn, deal +1d6 damage when you have Advantage on the attack, or an ally is engaging the target at close range.
  • Level 1 — Ghost Step: Difficult terrain does not slow you when moving cautiously or while hidden.
  • Level 5 — Tradecraft: Once per scene, reroll a failed Stealth, Hacking, or Deceive check.
  • Level 9 — Fade Out: When an enemy misses you, you may immediately move to nearby cover or attempt to Hide as a Reaction.

Operative Paths

Infiltrator — Level 3 False Face: Gain Advantage on Deceive checks involving disguise, cover identity, forged access, or impersonation.
Infiltrator — Level 7 Silent Entry: You may Hide as a bonus action, and the first attack you make from hiding deals +1d6 extra damage.
Infiltrator — Level 10 Untouchable: Once per scene, you may automatically pass through a guarded checkpoint, surveillance gap, or locked access point if there is any plausible method to do so. The GM decides the cost or consequence, but you get through.
Slicer — Level 3 Fast Slice: Your first Hacking attempt each scene is a bonus action.
Slicer — Level 7 System Puppet: Once per scene, on a successful Hacking check, you may do one extra thing for free: open a door, disable a camera, lock a target out, spoof credentials, or pull simple data.
Slicer — Level 10 Ghost in the Grid: You cannot be trivially traced through local systems. When you trigger a digital alarm, you may cause it to be delayed, misdirected, or partially concealed.
Saboteur — Level 3 Demolitions Kit: You can prepare charges, traps, and disruptions from common gear. Gain Advantage on rolls to breach, sabotage, or disable infrastructure.
Saboteur — Level 7 Chain Reaction: When you damage machinery, generators, doors, or vehicles, roll +1d8 damage and create a complication for enemies nearby.
Saboteur — Level 10 Planned Collapse: Once per mission, declare that you prepared a fallback sabotage point earlier. It may be an explosive line, severed support, blackout trigger, or hidden trap, subject to the GM's approval.
Marshal
Officers Rebel leaders Diplomats Field commanders Front-liners

Trained Skills: Command, Charm, Ranged, Discipline.

  • Level 1 — Command Ally: As a bonus action, choose one ally who can see or hear you. They may move a short distance or gain +2 on their next roll before the end of their next turn.
  • Level 1 — Hold the Line: Allies near you gain +1 on Discipline checks and saves against fear, panic, or suppression.
  • Level 5 — Field Presence: Once per round, when an ally within sight misses, fails a Discipline check, or is forced into cover, you may let them reroll.
  • Level 9 — Stand Fast: At the start of a fight, choose up to three allies. Until one of them drops to 0 Health, each chosen ally gains +1 Defense while they can see you.

Marshal Paths

Officer — Level 3 Coordinated Fire: If two allies attack the same target after you call the shot, the second gains +2 to hit.
Officer — Level 7 Tactical Shift: Once per round, move one ally into nearby cover or let them swap places with another ally.
Officer — Level 10 Battle Rhythm: The first time each round an ally spends Edge, they regain it if the action directly follows your command.
Diplomat — Level 3 Voice of Reason: Gain Advantage on Charm when calming tension, securing passage, or persuading uncertain neutrals.
Diplomat — Level 7 Read the Room: Once per scene, ask the GM who has leverage, what people fear, or which deal would work here.
Diplomat — Level 10 Ceasefire: Once per mission, if violence has not yet fully broken out, you may force a short negotiation pause unless a major villain chooses to break it publicly.
Vanguard — Level 3 Shield Advance: While in medium or heavy armor, adjacent allies gain +1 Defense.
Vanguard — Level 7 Intercept: Reaction. When an ally within reach is hit, move adjacent and take the hit instead, reducing damage by your Will or armor value.
Vanguard — Level 10 Unbroken Front: Once per scene, you and nearby allies ignore fear, suppression, and forced movement for one full round.
Gearhead
Mechanics Medics Techs Drone operators Field support

Trained Skills: Engineering, Hacking, Medicine, and one of Notice or Pilot.

  • Level 1 — Bypass: Gain +2 on Hacking or Engineering checks to bypass locks, doors, seals, or security.
  • Level 1 — Patch Kit: Once per Short Rest, restore 1d8 + Tech Health to an ally or repair 1 point of armor wear.
  • Level 5 — Field Fix: As an Action, remove one of the following from a nearby ally or item: jammed weapon, disabled gear, armor wear, light system failure, med-complication chosen by GM.
  • Level 9 — Always a Tool for It: Once per scene, produce a plausible technical tool, spare part, injector, cable, sealant, or interface from your kit.

Gearhead Paths

Mechanic — Level 3 Jury-Rig: In minutes rather than hours, you can make unstable but useful repairs that last until the end of the mission.
Mechanic — Level 7 Overclock: Once per scene, boost a weapon, tool, or vehicle for one roll with Advantage or +1 damage die.
Mechanic — Level 10 Master Retrofit: A vehicle, armor suit, or signature device you maintain gains one permanent custom module approved by the GM.
Medic — Level 3 Combat Stabilizer: Stabilizing a Downed ally is a bonus action for you.
Medic — Level 7 Stim Surge: Once per scene, give an ally +2 Initiative, +2 Move, or immediate recovery of 1 Resolve.
Medic — Level 10 Trauma Expert: The first time each mission an ally would gain a Wound, they do not.
Dronewright — Level 3 Support Drone: You gain a small drone. Each round it may grant +2 to one Notice, Hacking, or Engineering check, or deliver a small item.
Dronewright — Level 7 Armed Chassis: Your drone can make a light attack, apply a shock, or project a sensor mark once per round.
Dronewright — Level 10 Networked Control: Your drone can act at long range and can maintain two simple tasks at once.

  • Level 5 — Deep Attunement: Learn 1 additional Psionic power and gain +1 on Psionic rolls while you still have at least 1 Resolve.

Adept Paths

Section 9

Talents

Talents may be taken whenever your level grants one, or when the GM offers one as a special reward.

Combat Talents

  • Deadeye: Ignore Half Cover with ranged attacks.
  • Duelist: Gain +1 Defense while wielding a one-handed melee weapon with your off-hand empty.
  • Two-Gun Style: When attacking with a Light pistol, make one off-hand shot at -2.
  • Executioner: Deal +2 damage to wounded targets.
  • Quick Draw: Draw or ready a weapon for free. Gain Advantage on Initiative if combat starts suddenly.
  • Suppressive Fire: When using Autofire, targets struck suffer -2 on their next attack unless they take cover.
  • Close Quarters: No penalty for firing ranged weapons while engaged in melee.
  • Heavy Hitter: Once per turn, reroll one melee damage die.
  • Breacher: Ignore 1 point of armor when using shotguns, heavy melee weapons, or breaching tools.
  • Pinning Shot: When you spend Edge on a ranged attack, the target must also choose: drop prone, move to cover, or lose their Reaction.

Mobility Talents

  • Zero-G Training: Ignore penalties in low gravity or zero gravity.
  • Escape Artist: Gain Advantage to break restraints, cuffs, or grapples.
  • Parkour: Climbing and vaulting do not cost extra movement.
  • Jet Discipline: Gain Advantage when using jump packs, grav rigs, or aerial harnesses.
  • Combat Roll: Once per round, after taking ranged damage, move a short distance to cover if any is available.

Social Talents

  • Cool Under Fire: Gain Advantage on Discipline against fear, interrogation, or intimidation.
  • Silver Tongue: Once per scene, reroll a failed Charm or Deceive check.
  • Underworld Contacts: At the start of each mission, ask the GM for one black-market lead, rumor, or safehouse contact.
  • Authority Voice: Gain +2 Command when ordering low-tier NPCs, guards, or frightened crowds.
  • Sympathetic Face: Gain Advantage when trying to win trust from civilians, refugees, or uncertain recruits.

Tech Talents

  • Field Repair: Repair armor or a damaged weapon during combat as an Action.
  • Fast Slice: Your first successful Hacking check each scene also gives one minor extra benefit chosen by the GM.
  • Drone Link: Gain a small support drone if you do not already have one. If you already have a drone, improve its durability or range.
  • Cyber Affinity: Increase your safe Cyber Load by 1.
  • Tinkerer: During downtime, mod one weapon, gadget, or armor piece without needing outside help.

Psionic Talents

  • Psionic Adept: Learn one extra Psionic power.
  • Defensive Flow: Once per round after using a Psionic power, gain +2 Defense until the start of your next turn.
  • Psionic Leap Mastery: Leap costs no Resolve once per scene.
  • Dark Pull: When you defeat a target with a Psionic power, recover 1 Resolve but take 1 Strain.
  • Centered Breath: Once per scene, remove 1 Strain as an Action.
  • Quiet Presence: Gain Advantage on checks to hide or conceal your use of Psionics.

  • Shock Net: On a hit, target must beat Athletics 14 or become Restrained.
  • Breach Charge: Destroys locks, doors, weak walls, and light vehicles.

Section 14

GM Guide

Scene pacing, balance guidance, campaign pressure, and downtime.

GM-facing

What the GM Does

The GM describes the world, plays NPCs, sets difficulties, tracks Threat, and decides consequences. A good GM in this game keeps things moving and treats action as cinematic but dangerous.

Setting Difficulties

  • 10 for something routine under pressure
  • 12 for competent opposition or a decent lock
  • 15 for skilled resistance or dangerous terrain
  • 18 for elite guards, military systems, or advanced maneuvers
  • 22 for legendary feats or impossible odds

Using Threat Well

Threat should complicate, not negate.

  • the alarm triggers after the players succeed
  • a patrol rounds the corner at the worst time
  • the bounty target flees through a crowd
  • the locked door opens, but the hall beyond is occupied
  • the players get the data, but the trace is active

Bad uses: undoing success entirely, nullifying smart preparation every time, or punishing players for taking risks in a genre built on risks.

Encounter Building

  • 1 Standard enemy per PC = fair fight
  • 2 Minions = 1 Standard
  • 1 Elite = 2 Standards
  • 1 Boss = 3–4 Standards

A strong encounter often includes some cover, one time pressure or objective, one enemy with a special role, and one complication if the fight drags on.

Improvised NPC Formula

  • Weak NPC: Health 6, Defense 11, Attack +3, Damage 1d6
  • Trained NPC: Health 10, Defense 13, Attack +4, Damage 1d8
  • Dangerous NPC: Health 16, Defense 14, Attack +5, Damage 1d8+2
  • Elite NPC: Health 22, Defense 15, Attack +6, Damage 1d10+2, one Reaction or special rule

Add an identity such as sniper, duelist, commander, slicer, heavy gunner, Psionic user, assassin, riot trooper, or elite guard.

Heat and Reputation

Reputation: Track separately with major groups: −2 enemy, −1 hostile, 0 neutral, +1 friendly, +2 allied.

Heat: 0 unknown, 1 noticed, 2 watched, 3 hunted, 4 sector alert, 5 kill/capture priority.

Increase Heat when the crew acts publicly, leaves witnesses, destroys expensive property, attacks a major faction, or botches a clean escape. Reduce Heat when the crew changes routes, identities, or ships, lays low, frames others, bribes officials, or wins local protection.

Captures and Prisoners

When a target reaches 0 Health, the attacker may declare kill, incapacitate, or capture.

A target can only be captured cleanly if they are restrained, stunned, surprised, downed, or fully overpowered narratively. Bosses may require special cuffs, sedation, disabling cyberware, or multiple successful checks.

Downtime

Between missions, allow characters to repair gear, install cyberware, buy or trade equipment, lower Heat, gather intel, cultivate contacts, train a talent narratively, and upgrade a ship. Downtime is where rebellion cells grow and bounty networks react.

Section 15

Species and Character Identity

Identity-rich species design without turning species into a second class system.

Species in Remnants of the Far Galaxy

Species are meant to be important for identity, appearance, culture, and roleplay, but not so mechanically heavy that they overshadow Callings, Talents, gear, or Psionics.

Default Assumption

  • most people across the galaxy are mechanically similar enough to use the same core character rules
  • species differences are usually represented through one small trait, one situational edge, and fictional permissions
  • your Calling matters more than your biology for combat power and class role

This keeps the game easy to balance and easy to learn.

The Default Species Rule

Each species grants:

  • one minor trait
  • one flavor feature or movement/sense adjustment
  • sometimes one complication, social hook, or environmental note

Species should not usually grant

  • big Attribute bonuses
  • extra full actions
  • large damage boosts
  • multiple stacked combat powers
  • broad immunity to whole sections of the game

A species should feel meaningful without becoming a second class system.

Building a Species

A simple species write-up should answer four things:

  • What do they look or feel like in play?
  • What are they slightly better at?
  • What is one interesting limitation, social complication, or vulnerability?
  • How do they shape roleplay without replacing the character's Calling?
Sample Species Frameworks
Baseline Human

Trait — Adaptable: Gain one extra trained skill of your choice at character creation.

Humans are the baseline for the system and are intentionally flexible.

Agile Lithe Species

Trait — Quick Balance: Gain Advantage on checks to keep footing, squeeze through tight spaces, or avoid being knocked prone.

These species are suited to scouts, operatives, and pilots, but this bonus stays small enough not to define the whole character.

Large Hardy Species

Trait — Massive Frame: Gain +2 on Athletics checks to shove, carry, break, or hold position.

Drawback: Standard civilian gear, seats, or disguises may be awkward or costly to adapt.

This gives presence and utility without just handing out raw combat dominance.

Keen-Sense Species

Trait — Sharp Senses: Gain +2 to one kind of Notice check chosen at creation, such as scent, low-light vision, vibration, or distant sound.

This should help with scouting and tracking without invalidating the Notice skill.

Amphibious or Vacuum-Adapted Species

Trait — Environmental Tolerance: Choose one environment your species handles unusually well, such as underwater, toxic air, high cold, thin atmosphere, or low gravity.

This works best as a narrative advantage that occasionally provides Advantage or ignores a penalty.

Long-Lived Mystic Species

Trait — Deep Memory: Gain Advantage on one kind of Lore check tied to history, ancient rituals, or relics.

This supports tone and background, especially for Psionic traditions, without making them stronger in battle by default.

Small Species

Trait — Unassuming: Gain Advantage to hide in crowds, slip into maintenance spaces, or conceal yourself behind partial cover.

Drawback: Some heavy weapons, armor, mounts, and brute-Psionic tasks are harder to use at the GM's discretion.

Synthetic or Artificial Species

Trait — Constructed Mind: Gain Advantage against poison, disease, and some fear effects.

Drawback: Healing may require Engineering instead of Medicine in some cases, and anti-tech weapons may affect you.

This version works for droid-like or synthetic characters without making them immune to too much.

Species and Attributes

By default, species do not change Attribute modifiers.

Light Adjustment Rule

A species may shift one Attribute up by +1 and one different Attribute down by -1.

This keeps balance tight and avoids runaway optimization.

No Adjustment Rule

Species provide only traits and narrative identity.

This is the easiest version to run and is the default recommendation.

Species and Psionics

Species do not usually grant Psionic powers by default.

A species may have stronger traditions around Psionics, myths, taboos, or training styles, or unusual ways of perceiving Psionics.

But actual Psionic use should still come from the Adept Calling, a rare talent, or special training allowed by the GM.

That keeps Psionics special and prevents species choice from deciding your whole build.

Species and Equipment

Species shape what gear looks like more than what gear does.

  • larger species may need custom armor
  • smaller species may conceal weapons more easily
  • nonhuman body shapes may change how cyberware or clothing is fitted
  • synthetic characters may use different repair or healing procedures

Most of the time, keep this cosmetic or situational unless the story makes it important.

Best Practice for Play: Treat species as a strong roleplay anchor, a source of one memorable mechanical trait, and a way to imply galactic variety. Do not make species the main source of power. In this game, a Hunter is dangerous because they are a Hunter, an Operative is dangerous because they are an Operative, and a Psionic user is dangerous because they have training and Resolve. That keeps the game readable and fair.

Player Rules Summary

Roll 1d20 + Attribute + Skill. Beat the Difficulty or Defense. Succeed by 5+ or roll a natural 20 to gain Edge. Fail by 5+ or roll a natural 1 and the GM gains Threat.

On Your Turn

1 Action, 1 Move, 1 Reaction.

Common Actions

Attack, Dash, Aim, Hide, Assist, Use Item, Hack, Stabilize, Use Psionic, Ready.

Damage and Death

At 0 Health, you are Downed. Roll 1d20 each turn. 10+ hold on. 9 or less gain 1 Wound. At 3 Wounds, you die.

Edge and Threat

Edge boosts player momentum. Threat fuels complications and escalation.

Edge Uses

  • +1d6 damage
  • extra movement
  • disarm / shove / trip / pin
  • gain cover
  • help ally
  • reduce time
  • avoid collateral
  • spot useful detail
  • avoid weapon jam

Threat Uses

  • alarm triggered
  • reinforcements
  • bad position
  • escape route closes
  • social suspicion
  • weapon overheats
  • target flees
  • collateral danger

Section 17

Final Guidance for Play

The tone and pacing the game wants at the table.

This game runs best when

  • every mission has a goal, a complication, and a consequence
  • the players are allowed to be competent and bold
  • the GM uses Threat to escalate scenes, not block them
  • Heat and Reputation matter between sessions
  • Psionics feels rare, dangerous, and emotional
  • blaster fights include movement, cover, and changing objectives
Play Principle

If you are unsure how to rule something, choose the faster option that keeps the scene moving.

Remnants of the Far Galaxy should feel like a story about survivors and strivers living in the ruins of greater powers, where a single crew can still change the fate of worlds.
